Introduction to Universal Design's Core Philosophy

Making environments usable by all people regardless of age, size, or disability status.

Benefits extend beyond those with disabilities to all users.

Promotes equal access and opportunity.

The 7 Principles of Universal Design (1996)

  • Equitable use
  • Flexibility in use
  • Simple intuitive use (KISS principle)
  • Perceptible information
  • Tolerance for error
  • Size and space for approach and use

Detailed Breakdown of Diversity in Abilities

  • Visual impairments (blindness, low vision, color blindness)
  • Auditory impairments (deafness, hard of hearing)
  • Cognitive/Learning/Neurological challenges (invisible disabilities)
  • Physical limitations (mobility, dexterity)
  • Speech impairments
